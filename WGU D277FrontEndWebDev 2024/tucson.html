About Tucson

Tucson is the second largest city in the state of Arizona. It is home to the University of Arizona, which is also the city's largest employer (Wikipedia Contributors, 2023). US Army Fort Huachuca is also a large employer in the area, which has attracted technology and aerospace companies to the area (such as Raytheon, Texas Instruments, IBM, Honeywell Aerospace, etc). Tucson is another popular tourist destination, including home to the annual Tucson Gem and Mineral Show. You can learn more by visiting the City of Tucson website.

Urban Night Sky Place

Tucson is also known as a "dark sky place" and is a phenomenal place to seek out a good spot to go stargazing...or learn astrophotography! Saguaro National Park has been certified as an Urban Night Sky Place by the International Dark Sky Association (National Park Service, n.d.). This is awarded to public or private land that has "exceptional or distinguished" quality of starry nights, efforts to reduce light pollution including a protected nocturnal environment. DarkSky International is a nonprofit organization dedicated to preventing light pollution and promoting environmentally friendly practices, and was founded in Tucson Arizona. You can learn more by going to DarkSky International website.
