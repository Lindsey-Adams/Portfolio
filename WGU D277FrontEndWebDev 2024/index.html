About Arizona

Arizona is the 48th state, located in the Southwest geographical region of the United States. Phoenix is the capitol city. Arizona is part of the Four Corners region with Utah, Colorado, and New Mexico. Nevada, California, and the Mexican states Sonora and Baja California are it's other neighboring states. It's climate varies between it's northern and southern areas. Southern Arizona is known for its desert climate as a part of the Sonoran Desert, mountains, and hot summers and mild winters. Northern Arizona is known for its evergreen forests, canyons and other land formations, mountains, and moderate summer temperatures and significant snowfall in winters. Arizona is notable for its dramatic population growth since the 1950s. Arizona is also notable for its diverse population, including Hispanic peoples as well as Indigenous Americans. (Wikipedia Contributors, 2019)

Indingenous Americans

Arizona is home to 22 Indigenous American tribes and at least 17 reservations. Click here to see a list of the Indigenous American tribes in Arizona. There are many state and national parks dedicated to historical monuments, communities, and geographical locations such as the Wupatki National Monument. Sites such as these continue to educate and preserve the rich history of Arizona and early Indigenous Americans.


Travel

Arizona has many amazing travel destinations for all ages and interest. You can spend at least a weekend enjoying this amazing state from the Grand Canyon to the Sonoran desert, to golfing at a resort in the shadows of Camelback mountain, or to the culturally diverse food and entertainment.
